# Content Differentiation Rules

Every page must have a distinct lens. A reader visiting two pages must NOT feel like they're reading the same article with different keywords swapped in.

**Why:** The 9 content pages share the same product, same proof points, and same buyer personas. Without strict differentiation rules, every page will open with "orders arrive in messy formats," explain the same 5-step process, cite the same Meesenburg numbers, and address the same "burned before" objection. This kills user engagement and signals thin content to Google.

**How to apply:** Before writing any page, check what that page OWNS and what it must NOT repeat. Every section below is mandatory.

## Page Ownership Map

| Page | Owns This Lens | Opens With | Does NOT Cover |
|------|---------------|------------|----------------|
| EN-P1 (sales order automation) | Breadth overview, full "How It Works" | Distribution order desk reality | Email mechanics, AI tech, ROI math, PO specifics |
| EN-P2 (email order processing) | Email inbox as order channel | Format diversity WITHIN email | General automation, AI comparison, ROI, POs |
| EN-P3 (ai order processing) | AI technology, OCR/RPA comparison | The technology gap | General problem, email specifics, ROI math, POs |
| EN-P4 (order processing automation) | ROI, business case, cost math | The numbers ($750K/year in errors) | AI tech, email specifics, PO specifics |
| EN-P5 (purchase order automation) | PO receiving vs procurement tools | Search intent mismatch (Coupa vs receiving) | General automation, email, AI tech, ROI math |
| EN-CS1 (Meesenburg) | The FULL case study story | Challenge at Meesenburg | Product explanations, technical details, ROI framework |
| EN-B1 (what is sales order automation) | Educational deep-dive, definitions | The concept (educational frame) | Product pitch, AI comparison, email specifics |
| EN-B2 (how AI processes email orders) | Technical step-by-step walkthrough | A concrete example email | What-is education, product pitch, ROI |
| EN-B3 (complete guide OPA) | Comparison and decision framework | The decision the reader faces | What-is education, AI walkthrough, product pitch |
| EN-B4 (how to automate PO processing) | How-to guide, receiving-side PO focus | Search intent mismatch (procurement vs receiving) | Full AI tech walkthrough, full comparison matrix, full Meesenburg narrative, ROI framework |

## Meesenburg Rule (CRITICAL)

- **EN-CS1:** Full story, named testimonial, deployment details, before/after. The ONLY page with the complete narrative.
- **All other pages:** 1-2 sentences referencing 98%/50% numbers + link to /insights/meesenburg-order-automation. No quoting Banciu Nicolae. No retelling the story. No describing the implementation.

## "How OrderFlow Works" Rule

- **EN-P1:** Gets the FULL 5-step process (monitor > interpret > match > flag > output)
- **All other product pages:** 2-3 sentence summary with link to EN-P1
- **EN-B2:** Gets the detailed TECHNICAL walkthrough (step-by-step with examples, different from EN-P1's product-oriented version)

## "Burned Before" Objection Rule

Each page handles it through its OWN lens:
- EN-P1: Direct objection handling ("OCR/EDI/RPA failed, here's why")
- EN-P2: Email-specific ("email automation routes emails, it doesn't READ them")
- EN-P3: Technology comparison (the comparison table IS the objection handler)
- EN-P4: Cost framing ("the sunk cost isn't the license, it's the 6 months configuring templates")
- EN-P5: PO-specific ("procurement tools solve the sender's problem, not yours")
- EN-B1: Educational framing (maturity model, not blame)
- EN-B2: Demonstrated through the walkthrough (show, don't tell)
- EN-B3: Built into the comparison framework
- EN-B4: Template trap framing ("starting with templates instead of AI is the most expensive mistake")

## Reader Flow

```
TOFU: EN-B1 (what is this?) > EN-B3 (which approach?)
MOFU: EN-B2 (how does AI work?) > EN-B4 (how to automate POs?) > EN-P3 (why AI?)
BOFU: EN-P1/P2/P4/P5 (product pages) > EN-CS1 (proof) > /contact (convert)
```
